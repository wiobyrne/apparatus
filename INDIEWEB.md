# IndieWeb and Apparatus

Apparatus and IndieWeb overlap, but they are not the same thing.

Apparatus is the reading interface system. IndieWeb is the ownership and
publishing philosophy that can support it.

## Core

These IndieWeb ideas fit comfortably inside Apparatus:

- own your domain and identity
- publish on your own site first
- keep visible data human-first
- make what you need
- use what you make
- document your stuff
- keep permalinks stable
- make RSS or another readable feed available when it helps readers
- keep bylines, about pages, and contact surfaces clear

## Optional

These are useful tools or integrations, but they do not define Apparatus:

- POSSE
- Webmention
- Micropub
- IndieAuth
- `rel=me`
- syndication bridges
- response backfeed

## Out of scope

These should not be treated as required Apparatus behavior:

- social reply mechanics as a page requirement
- like/repost/favorite systems as a core pattern
- federation as the visual language of the site
- any specific IndieWeb stack

## Practical rule

If an IndieWeb feature helps readers find, trust, cite, subscribe to, or move
content without losing ownership, it is a good candidate for Apparatus.

If it mainly adds social mechanics or platform mimicry, it should stay
optional.
