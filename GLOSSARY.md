# Glossary

This note defines the core terms Apparatus uses consistently.

## Apparatus

A portable reading interface framework for digital publishing.

It translates print habits into pixel spaces so pages stay readable, oriented,
and human across surfaces.

## Reading-first

A design posture where reading is the primary job of the page.

If a change does not improve reading, orientation, or navigation, it does not
belong.

## Interface grammar

The rules that govern how a page is put together:

- hierarchy
- typography
- metadata
- links
- spacing
- tone

## Surface

A destination where Apparatus is expressed in a specific medium.

## Surface translation

The process of keeping the reading contract stable while changing the surface
expression to fit the medium.

## Reading contract

The stable expectations a reader can count on across Apparatus surfaces:

- readable hierarchy
- clear orientation
- selective linking
- calm composition
- accessible behavior

## Pattern

A reusable structural form that repeats across pages or surfaces.

## Anti-pattern

A pattern Apparatus explicitly avoids because it undermines reading.

## Canonical

The notes that define the stable system truth.

Canonical notes should be short, specific, and resistant to drift.

## Implementation history

The record of how Apparatus changed in a live implementation or other working
system.

## Temporary exploration

Ideas worth testing that are not yet part of the stable system truth.

## Additive

The preferred method for updating Apparatus.

New findings should extend the system without erasing the reasoning trail.
