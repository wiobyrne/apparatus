# About Apparatus

Apparatus is a way of designing digital spaces so they feel easier to read.

It is built around a simple idea: when people land on a page, they should be
able to tell what it is, how to move through it, and what matters without
having to fight the interface.

## What it does

Apparatus helps a page do a few basic jobs well:

- show readers where they are
- make the content easy to scan and read
- keep the layout calm and predictable
- let the page work across different media

## Where it can be used

Apparatus is meant to move across different kinds of publishing spaces:

- websites
- notes and digital gardens
- newsletters
- slide decks
- diagrams and teaching visuals

## What makes it different

Apparatus is not trying to be flashy. It is trying to be useful.

It keeps a small set of design rules and uses them consistently so the page
feels like a readable place instead of a cluttered interface.

## In one sentence

Apparatus is a portable reading framework for digital publishing.
